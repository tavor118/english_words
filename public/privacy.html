Privacy Policy

Last updated: 8 May 2026

English Words ("the app") is a free, open-source vocabulary-learning web app hosted on GitHub Pages. This policy explains what information the app handles and how it is used.

Summary

  • The app runs entirely in your browser. There is no backend server operated by us.
  • By default, your word list is stored only in your browser's local storage.
  • If you choose to sign in with Google, your word list is synced to a hidden folder inside your own Google Drive. We never see, store, or transmit your data.
  • We do not use analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking.

Information the app handles

The app stores the vocabulary data you create: words, translations, example sentences, tags, image URLs, audio URLs, favorite status, review timestamps, and review counters. This data exists only on devices and accounts you control:

  • Browser local storage on the device where you use the app.
  • Your Google Drive (only if you sign in for sync), in a single file named words.json inside the hidden appDataFolder. This folder is not visible in the regular Google Drive interface and is accessible only to this app.

The app does not collect names, email addresses, contact lists, or any other personal information. When you sign in with Google, the access token issued by Google stays in your browser; it is never transmitted to us.

Google API Services — Limited Use disclosure

English Words' use and transfer of information received from Google APIs to any other app will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy , including the Limited Use requirements.

Specifically, the app:

  • Requests only the drive.appdata scope, which grants access to a hidden, per-app folder in the user's Drive — not to any other files.
  • Uses Google user data solely to provide the user-facing sync feature (read and write one JSON file containing the user's own vocabulary list).
  • Does not transfer Google user data to third parties except as needed to provide or improve the sync feature, comply with applicable law, or as part of a merger or sale (none of which currently apply).
  • Does not use Google user data for advertising purposes.
  • Does not allow humans to read Google user data unless we have the user's affirmative agreement for specific messages, doing so is necessary for security purposes, to comply with applicable law, or the data has been aggregated and anonymized.

Third-party services the app may contact from your browser

To enrich vocabulary entries, the app makes requests directly from your browser to a small set of public APIs. These requests contain only the word you are looking up — no account identifiers, no personal information:

  • MyMemory — automatic translation
  • Free Dictionary API — pronunciation audio
  • Datamuse — spelling suggestions
  • Wikipedia — illustrative image search
  • Google Drive API — sync (only after you sign in)

Each of these services has its own privacy policy. Browser-issued requests will include standard metadata such as your IP address and user-agent string.

Cookies and tracking

The app sets no cookies and uses no analytics or advertising trackers. Google's sign-in flow may set its own cookies on accounts.google.com as part of the OAuth process.

Data retention and deletion

You can remove your data at any time:

  • Local data — clear your browser's storage for this site, or use your device's site settings.
  • Drive sync data — sign out from inside the app, then revoke the app's access at myaccount.google.com/permissions . You can also delete the appDataFolder contents from Drive Settings → Manage apps.

Children

The app is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

Changes to this policy
